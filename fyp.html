Final Year Project: Soc-Now







Abstract

In todayâ€™s era of Technological superiority, almost every work is automated and in its automation the major part is played by System on Chip (SoC). Designing a verified SoC from scratch takes months to complete, sometimes years to design an efficient one. In order to save time designers prefer to re-use a verified and well documented component in their design which ends up more time consuming. SoC-Now is a Final Year Project and Research Work of Undergraduate Students of BSc Software Engineering Domain. It is an open source software suite that can generate or design a System on Chip (SoC) by an automated process. It will facilitate hardware designers to design and generate an SoC or a single component of it by just selecting their desired specifications. The components will be plug and play supported. The SoC-Now journey starts from Micro Electronic Research Lab (MERL) which is working in the field of RISC V Processor Development and Research. SoC-Now is a System on Chip (SoC) generator developed in Scala and CHISEL that follows the programming paradigm of Functional Programming to achieve reusability. The main purpose is to generate the bitstream of an SoC of required specifications and generate reusable SoC components separately. Additionally, the SoC and its components will be tested and verified by use of a separate Verification Module.
